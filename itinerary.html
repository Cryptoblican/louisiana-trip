Old State Capitol — Afternoon
📍 100 North Blvd, Baton Rouge
Gothic castle on the Mississippi River. Mark Twain called it a "monstrosity" — and he wasn't entirely wrong, but it's stunning. Restored museum of Louisiana political history. Genuinely strange and beautiful, and easy to fit in after church before hitting the road south.

Atchafalaya Basin — Deep Bayou
The largest swamp in America. Cypress trees, gators, Spanish moss, total immersion.

Labor Day note: Most bayou tour operators run on Labor Day — it's actually a popular weekend for locals. Poche's Market may have limited hours. Call ahead to confirm. Everything else should be normal.

Sunrise on the Houseboat Deck
📍 Atchafalaya Basin, Breaux Bridge area
Set your alarm. The bayou at sunrise — mist rising off the water, birds starting up, total silence — is one of those moments. Don't sleep through it.
🐊
Atchafalaya Basin Boat Tour — McGee's Lodge
📍 Henderson, LA · ~30 min from Breaux Bridge
The largest river swamp in the United States — 1.4 million acres. McGee's runs small-group tours through ancient cypress forests draped in Spanish moss. You'll see alligators, herons, egrets, roseate spoonbills. Go in the morning when wildlife is most active. Book ahead — Labor Day fills up.
📞 Book in Advance
🥩
Poche's Market & Restaurant
📍 3015 Main Hwy, Breaux Bridge, LA
Cash-only Cajun butcher and deli. This is not a tourist place — it's where locals have been getting their boudin, cracklins, smoked sausage, and stuffed mirliton for decades. The boudin alone is worth the trip to Breaux Bridge. Get extra.
🚣
Kayak the Bayou Teche Corridor
📍 Bayou Teche, Breaux Bridge area
Bayou Teche winds through Cajun country for 125 miles. Kayak rentals available in the area. Go at your own pace through cypress-lined waterways, past old fishing camps and boat shacks. The afternoon light on the water is incredible.
🍤
Robin's Restaurant — Dinner
📍 Henderson, LA
No pretense, no tourists — just the crawfish étouffée that locals drive an hour for. Unpretentious Cajun cooking at its finest. Order the étouffée, the fried catfish, or whatever the special is. You won't leave disappointed.

Evening Activity — Haunted History Ghost Tour
📍 French Quarter, New Orleans · Departs near St. Louis Cathedral
New Orleans has been running ghost tours since the city was young — and for good reason. Haunted History Tours is the original operator (since 1994): ~2 hours walking the French Quarter, covering voodoo, yellow fever epidemics, Madame LaLaurie, and the city's genuinely dark past. Atmospheric, historically grounded, and a perfect first-night intro to NOLA. Book tickets in advance — they sell out.

Alternatives: Steamboat Natchez evening jazz cruise (departs 7pm from the Riverwalk) or Preservation Hall jazz show (223 St. Peter St — intimate, legendary). Both require advance booking.
📞 Book in Advance
🏨

Wednesday · Sept 9
New Orleans — The Real Version
Tremé, jazz, legendary food, the Garden District. Skip the tourist traps.
☕
Congregation Coffee — Morning
📍 3109 Magazine St or Marigny location, New Orleans
Better coffee than Café Du Monde, infinitely less crowded. Local roaster. Start the day right before diving into the city.
🏘
Tremé Neighborhood Walk
📍 Tremé, New Orleans
The oldest African American neighborhood in the United States. The actual birthplace of jazz — not Bourbon Street. Walk it, feel the architecture, find the second-line culture. More authentic than anywhere in the French Quarter.
⚰️
St. Louis Cemetery #1
📍 400 Basin St, New Orleans
Above-ground tombs, Napoleon's would-be resting place, voodoo history, genuinely eerie atmosphere. Guided tour now required to enter (Save Our Cemeteries runs them — book ahead). One of the most unique spots in America.
📞 Guided Tour Required
🍗
Dooky Chase's Restaurant — Lunch
📍 2301 Orleans Ave, Tremé, New Orleans
Legendary institution. Leah Chase fed Martin Luther King Jr., Ray Charles, and Barack Obama at this table. The fried chicken is a pilgrimage item. The gumbo z'herbes is unlike anything you've had. This is not just a meal — it's a piece of American history.
❤️ Do Not Miss
🏡
Garden District Stroll
📍 Garden District, New Orleans
Antebellum mansions, live oaks, Magazine Street shops. Commander's Palace is here — if you want to splurge on a late lunch or afternoon cocktail, the bar at Commander's is worth it. The bread pudding soufflé is absurd.
🍷
Bacchanal Wine — Evening
📍 600 Poland Ave, Bywater, New Orleans
Outdoor wine bar with live music in the courtyard. Grab a bottle from the shop inside, take it to the backyard, and listen to whatever trio is playing. Jen will love this. It's the perfect last-night vibe — relaxed, local, beautiful.
🎷
Frenchmen Street — Late Night
📍 Frenchmen St, Marigny, New Orleans
The Spotted Cat, d.b.a., Maison — walk between them, tip the musicians, stay as long as you want. This is what NOLA is actually about. Last night energy.

Café Du Monde — Early Morning Beignets
📍 800 Decatur St, French Quarter
You basically have to. Café au lait and three beignets. Powdered sugar everywhere. Go early (~7:30am) before the tourist crowds and before hitting the road to Destrehan.
🏛
Destrehan Plantation — Morning Tour
📍 13034 River Rd, Destrehan, LA · ~30 min from French Quarter · ~25 min from MSY
One of the oldest intact plantation homes in the lower Mississippi Valley, built in 1787. Massive live oaks, original outbuildings, and tours covering the full history including the 1811 German Coast Uprising — one of the largest slave rebellions in U.S. history, which ended on these grounds. Beautiful and sobering. Tours run ~1 hour — go for the first available slot (typically 9am or 9:30am). Reserve in advance.
📞 Reserve Ahead
